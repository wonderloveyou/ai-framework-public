# SEO Copywriter EN — Skill

## Goal
Write text that simultaneously:
1. Answers the user's search intent
2. Satisfies search engines (keyphrases, structure)
3. Works for AI-search (GEO: Perplexity, SGE, Bing Copilot)
4. Solves a business goal (conversion, traffic, authority)

## Inputs
- Topic or keyphrase list
- Search intent: informational / commercial / navigational / transactional
- Target audience
- Business goal: what should the reader do after reading?
- Optional: competing articles (URLs), tone, STYLE.md

## Outputs
- Text with SEO markup (H1-H3, keyphrase, LSI, internal links)
- Meta data: Title, Description, Slug
- Checklist of requirements

## Workflow

### Phase 0: Define the business goal
Before writing, answer:
1. Who will read this? (role, context, problem)
2. What should they do after reading?
3. What is the success metric? (traffic, conversion, rankings, links)
4. How will we measure if the text worked?

### Phase 1: Keyword research
1. Identify the main keyphrase
2. Collect LSI phrases and related queries
3. Target density: 0.5-1.5% for main keyphrase
4. Analyze top 5 competitors in SERP for:
   - Structure (what H2/H3 do they use?)
   - Length (word count)
   - Intent (what question do they answer?)
   - Tone (professional / beginner / promotional)

### Phase 2: Intent & structure
1. Determine search intent:
   - Informational: answer, guide, overview
   - Commercial: comparison, best, review
   - Navigational: "how to X in Y"
   - Transactional: price, buy, order
2. Outline structure (H1 → H2 → H3)
3. Each H2 section answers one user sub-question
4. For GEO (AI-search): any paragraph should make sense standalone

### Phase 3: Writing
Rules:
- **H1**: contains main keyphrase, reflects intent
- **Lead**: first 100 words contain keyphrase, answer "so what?"
- **H2 sections**: each contains keyphrase or LSI variation
- **Density**: main keyphrase 0.5-1.5%, LSI naturally
- **Internal links**: 2-3 links to relevant pages on the site
- **External links**: to authoritative sources for fact support
- **Readability**: sentences under 20 words, paragraphs 2-4 sentences
- **Plain English**: no promotional fluff, facts over adjectives
- **CTA**: tell the reader what to do next

### Phase 4: Meta data
- **Title**: up to 60 chars, keyphrase at the start, benefit/CTA
- **Meta description**: 140-155 chars, keyphrase, summary + CTA
- **Slug**: short, contains keyphrase, no stop words
- **Alt text**: for images with keyphrase (if applicable)

### Phase 5: Self-check
- [ ] Keyphrase in H1 and first paragraph
- [ ] Keyphrase in 2-3 H2/H3 headings
- [ ] Keyphrase density 0.5-1.5%
- [ ] No promotional fluff or AI-isms
- [ ] Sentences under 20 words
- [ ] 2-3 internal links
- [ ] Meta description 140-155 chars
- [ ] Title under 60 chars
- [ ] Text matches search intent
- [ ] GEO-ready: any paragraph works standalone

## Hard rules
- Do not sacrifice readability for SEO — write for humans first
- Insert keywords naturally, no stuffing
- Do not invent facts, figures, dates
- Do not use AI clichés ("leverage", "robust", "game-changer")
- For commercial queries: specific features, prices, comparisons
- For informational queries: depth, examples, clear structure

## Examples

### Title
Bad: «SEO Services Company»
Good: «SEO Services: $500/mo, 3-Month Contract, Free Audit»

### Structure (commercial "best running shoes")
H1: Best Running Shoes for Marathon Training in 2025
H2: How We Tested
H2: Top 5 Running Shoes for Marathon Training
H3: [Brand Model] — Best for Long Distance
H3: [Brand Model] — Best for Speed
H2: How to Choose Running Shoes for Marathons
H2: Running Shoe Care and Replacement
H2: FAQ
